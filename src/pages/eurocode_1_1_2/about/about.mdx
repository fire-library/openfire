# Parametric Fires

## Some Stuff About

Parametric fire curves in Eurocode 1 offer a method to represent the
thermal action of fires in a more "realistic" manner than
standard time-temperature curves (such as the ISO 834 fire curve).
These curves are based on the physical parameters of the fire
scenario, including the geometry of the compartment, ventilation
conditions, properties of the enclosure materials, and the
characteristics of the fire load.

The Heating phase gas temperature of a parametric fire is defined as:

```math
\Theta_g=20+1325(1-0.324e^{-0.2t^*}-0.204e^{-1.7t^*}-0.472e^{-19t^*})
```

Where:

$${\Theta_g}$$ is the gas temperature in the heating phase

$${t^*=t\cdot\Gamma}$$

```math
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{-x^2}\ dx
```
